NEW YORK, April 25 (Reuters) - The S&P 500 ended Thursday's session slightly lower as a dive in industrials stocks and concerns about slowing global growth overshadowed gains in Facebook Inc and Microsoft Corp.
The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 135.1 points, or 0.51%, to 26,461.95, the S&P 500 lost 1.09 points, or 0.04%, to 2,926.16 and the Nasdaq Composite added 16.67 points, or 0.21%, to 8,118.68. (Reporting by Sinad Carew Editing by James Dalgleish)
